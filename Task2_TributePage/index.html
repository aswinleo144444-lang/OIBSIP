About the King
Who is King Kohli?

Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. Raised in a middle-class family, cricket was never just a sport for him — it was an obsession, a calling, and eventually a legacy.

Known as King Kohli or Chase Master, Virat redefined what it means to bat under pressure. His ability to anchor chases while scoring at a furious pace made him arguably the greatest ODI batsman of all time.

Beyond numbers, Kohli's intensity, fitness culture, and passion transformed Indian cricket. He brought a new standard of fielding, athleticism, and aggression that inspired an entire generation of cricketers.

Career Timeline
2008
U19 World Cup — Captain & Champion
Led India to the U19 World Cup title in Malaysia. His aggressive captaincy and match-winning performances announced him to the world.
2008
ODI Debut
Made his ODI debut against Sri Lanka at age 19. Scored 12 — but the hunger and drive were unmistakable from the very first ball.
2011
World Cup Winner
Part of India's 2011 ODI World Cup winning squad. Scored a crucial 35 in the final and ran between wickets like a man possessed.
2013
Becomes #1 ODI Batsman
Reached the top of ICC ODI rankings — a position he would occupy for years. His consistency across all formats was now undeniable.
2017
Full Captain Across All Formats
Became India's captain across Tests, ODIs, and T20Is. Under him, India reached the #1 Test ranking and established dominance globally.
2023
ODI World Cup — 50th ODI Hundred
Broke Sachin Tendulkar's all-time ODI centuries record with his 50th hundred during the 2023 World Cup on home soil — a moment that made the nation stand still.
2024
T20 World Cup — Mission Complete
Played a match-winning knock in the T20 World Cup final against South Africa in Barbados. Announced T20I retirement after helping India win the title.
